## Test strategy — EditMode / PlayMode / shipped-build-capture split

- **EditMode (need math — `ThirstNeedTests`, mirror `HungerNeedTests`):** decay over a window via `TickSeconds`; floor rest + clamp (no overshoot past `floor01*max`); `AddWater` restore + clamp-to-max; `Changed` fires on decay AND on scoop; `Changed` does NOT fire on a no-op; `IsCritical` tracks threshold; `ApplyDifficulty` swaps the decay rate.
- **EditMode (world-gen determinism — mirror `RoundIslandNavTests` + scatter guards):** the pond is PRESENT in the seed-42 generation AND does NOT shift the island silhouette or move existing scatter (trees/rocks/bushes/sticks). World-gen runs editor-time (`LowPolyZoneGen`/`WorldBootstrap` live in `Assets/Scripts/Editor`) → presence + shape-invariance is EditMode-appropriate. Assert the EXISTING scatter set is byte-stable WITH the pond added (AC2a).
- **PlayMode (decay FIRES over a real `Time.time` window — the deltaTime≈0 trap; mirror `HungerNeedPlayModeTests`):** thirst measurably DROPS across a wall-clock window; `AddWater` restores end-to-end. **This is the ONLY layer that proves the `Update`→`Time.time`-accumulation wiring** — headless `deltaTime≈0` makes a per-frame decay green-pass EditMode and never tick in the build.
- **PlayMode (pond reachable + drink-scoop seam — owns the AC3a seam test):** pond is ON/near the NavMesh + reachable (mirror `RoundIslandNavCoveragePlayModeTests`); NavMesh-coverage % does NOT regress vs pre-pond. Drink-scoop near the pond → thirst +per-scoop AND `Changed` fires; repeatable (N scoops → N×amount up to max). **Negatives (load-bearing, AC3a):** a scoop FAR from the pond does NOTHING; NO inventory entry is created/consumed (thirst is NOT berries — drink is proximity+interact, not an item).
- **Shipped-build capture (mandatory — UX-visible world feature):** the pond visible IN the world, reading as FRESH water (NOT the salt sea); walk to it; drink-scoop raises the readout (the bar once `86caamkxv` lands, else a log trace). Boot clean (no `USER WARNING:`/`USER ERROR:`). **Grounding check (`verify-grounding-soaks-by-gameplay-cam-visual`):** open the PLAYER-FRAMING gameplay-cam capture and SEE the pond sits IN the terrain — metrics + top-down captures have lied before (served floating builds twice).

## SILENT-KILLER FLAGS to watch at QA

**1. Structural — the advisory PlayMode job is cancellable and may NOT execute (ticket `86cabfa21`, status `to do`).**
This is the #1 flag and it is NEW since the older draft. The advisory/decoupled `playmode` CI job is cancellable; a PlayMode suite riding it can SILENTLY fail to execute, so its assertions are never observed green on a run. It already bit `InventoryBeltHeldAxe` (PR #90, `86caa4bya`) and the `HungerNeed` PlayMode pair (PR #93, `86caamkp8`). The ticket EXPLICITLY names THIRST `86caamkv7` as a future suite that rides the same broken job. **At QA I must verify the ThirstNeed PlayMode suite — the real-window decay AND the drink-seam tests — ACTUALLY RAN on the PR head SHA (non-zero, observed green), not that the green came from a cancelled job.** A "CI green" with the PlayMode job skipped/cancelled is the execution-hole, NOT a pass. This is the exact class as the inventory-PlayMode gap: behavior is correct, the suite exists, but it didn't execute → the runtime-only guards (deltaTime≈0 decay wiring; proximity-gated scoop) are unproven on that run. If the slot is still advisory when thirst lands, I gate the runtime ACs on (a) the EditMode `TickSeconds` coverage + (b) the shipped-build capture, and flag the unobserved PlayMode suite explicitly in my verdict (mirror the #90/#93 QA notes), not approve it as silently green.

**2. Inheritance/surface drift (HUD contract — `86caamkxv`).** `ThirstNeed` must EXTEND `SurvivalNeed`, not re-declare its surface. A shadowed `Current01`/`Changed`/`TickSeconds` green-passes the need's own tests but forks the single HUD bind path. Grep-check: `class ThirstNeed : SurvivalNeed` + `AddWater` is the only new public satisfaction hook + no re-declared base members.

**3. `Changed` not firing on a real value change** — the subscribe-never-poll HUD freezes silently. Mirror `Changed_Fires_OnDecayAndSatisfaction` AND `Changed_DoesNotFire_OnNoOpChange`.

**4. Proximity bypass.** A drink that restores anywhere (no distance gate) passes the positive test and breaks the feature. ALWAYS test the FAR-from-pond negative (AC3a).

**5. Water-as-inventory-item regression.** Thirst is NOT berries — no inventory entry on a scoop. A copy-paste from the berry eat-action that routes water through inventory is a design break; assert inventory is untouched by a scoop.

**6. Seed-42 / NavMesh regression** (the world-shifts-under-everything killer). The pond must integrate WITHOUT re-rolling the seed, moving existing scatter, or shrinking NavMesh coverage. Pre/post island-shape + NavMesh-coverage invariance assertions; AC2a makes a forced re-roll a SPONSOR-GATE, not a silent merge.

**7. Floating pond** (`verify-grounding-soaks-by-gameplay-cam-visual`). Verify the pond sits IN the terrain via the player-framing gameplay-cam capture, not a top-down/metrics check (both lied before).
